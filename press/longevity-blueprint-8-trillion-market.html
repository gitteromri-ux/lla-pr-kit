eTeacher Group Launches Longevity Life Academy to Open the $8 Trillion Longevity Market to Everyday Americans

Between $2-million concierge clinics and free, unverified advice sits the mainstream American household no one serves well. eTeacher's edge isn't a lower price — it's a global-leading teaching engine now brought to healthspan, from $289 a month.

For Immediate Release

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE — eTeacher Group, a global leader in online education, is entering one of consumer health's fastest-growing categories, launching Longevity Life Academy, its sixth proprietary online school, to occupy a gap its CEO argues no one serves well: the space between expensive concierge clinics and free, unverified online advice. The gap isn't only between $2-million clinics and free advice, it's between information and follow-through.

The opportunity is large and split. UBS values the longevity market at $5.3 trillion in 2023, rising toward $8 trillion by 2030, while the narrower healthspan-product market is estimated at $25 billion-plus today, growing toward roughly $63 billion by 2035, per Market Research Future. Yet rigor, engagement, and accessibility rarely coexist in one offering. The clinics have rigor but no reach; the feeds have reach but no rigor; and neither solves the problem that defeats most consumers, staying with a program long enough for it to work.

eTeacher's real advantage is one rivals would take years to build: a teaching method proven to make learning stick. It has delivered 400,000+ courses across nearly 200 countries using live experts in small cohorts, guided daily practice, and a community that drives completion, with roughly 70% of its customers already in the US and a proven billing and retention engine behind it. That combination of live instruction, engagement, and dependable operations is difficult to assemble and rarer still in consumer health, where churn is the industry's defining weakness.

Its flagship program, the Longevity Blueprint, applies that method to healthspan. Delivered entirely online, it runs 18 weeks and 18 live sessions across four phases, with cohorts of 8 to 15 students moving from foundational principles to a written, personalized protocol spanning six pillars: Nutrition, Sleep, Exercise and Movement, Supplements and Wearables, Stress Management, and an individual Longevity Protocol. Each student receives an Abbott Lingo continuous glucose monitor, shipped to their US home before Lesson 5, so the curriculum is anchored in their own biomarker data rather than generic advice. Instruction comes from globally certified longevity instructors, including founding faculty member Julie Gibson Clark, who held the number two spot on the Rejuvenation Olympics leaderboard for more than a year, ahead of the contest's own founder Bryan Johnson. Everything is delivered through eTeacher's proprietary advanced learning management system, live classes, lesson recordings, daily practice, a community forum, and progress tracking in one platform, the same infrastructure behind the company's academic online schools.

Pricing starts at $289 per month, a fraction of concierge longevity programs that can run into six or seven figures. But eTeacher frames accessibility as more than affordability: the design goal is completion. A program an ordinary household can start, finish, and sustain is, in the company's view, the only kind that actually raises healthspan at scale.

eTeacher plans to broaden the catalog with additional longevity courses over the coming year and to add a companion practice app, deepening its position in the mainstream tier of a market it believes is still waiting for a credible middle.

"Longevity is where our vision meets the largest consumer opportunity of our time. The billionaires can afford to experiment. Our job is to take what is proven, teach it live, and make it something a working family can actually follow. That is why we put a real teacher in the room, you can experiment alone, or you can be taught, supported, and expected back. We built our whole school on the second one," said Harel Tayeb, CEO of eTeacher Group.
