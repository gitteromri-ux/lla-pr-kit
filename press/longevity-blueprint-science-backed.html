eTeacher Group Launches Longevity Life Academy: Science-Backed Longevity, Built on Your Numbers

The global online-education leader opens its sixth online school, a full academy for healthy aging taught live on its proprietary platform, making world-class longevity accessible: real teachers, a small class, a supportive community, and an Abbott Lingo glucose monitor included.

For Immediate Release

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE — Longevity has a reputation for being expensive, intimidating, and impossible to stick with. Longevity Life Academy, the new sixth school from global education leader eTeacher Group, is built to be the opposite: a guided protocol built around your own biology and your own numbers, taught live in groups of 8 to 15 by experts who know your name, with a community that makes the habits last.

Accessibility here is about more than cost. As the longevity market races toward $8 trillion by 2030 (UBS), its highest-profile figures spend fortunes chasing extra years, yet most people who try to follow along quit within weeks. LLA's answer is method, not money: the same live, community-driven approach that helped hundreds of thousands of eTeacher learners finish what they started, now aimed at healthy aging. Tuition starts from $289 per month.

Instead of memorizing facts about glucose or sleep, students watch those numbers move in their own bodies. Every US student receives an Abbott Lingo continuous glucose monitor before Lesson 5, then builds a personal protocol across the program's six pillars: Nutrition, Sleep, Exercise and Movement, Supplements and Wearables, Stress Management, and a personal Longevity Protocol, one small habit at a time. It is a plan meant to be kept for the next decades, not abandoned after a 10-week challenge. The Lingo device is a 14-day, painless biosensor, available without a prescription and FSA/HSA-eligible; it is intended for adults 18 and over, is not for people who use insulin, and is not a diagnostic tool.

The structure is deliberately human-scale, and it all lives online in one place. Over 18 weeks, an instructor who knows every student by name leads a weekly 50-minute live session, then short daily practices fill the gaps between classes, small enough to fit a working parent's schedule, specific enough to build real momentum. It runs on eTeacher's proprietary advanced learning platform, where students find lesson recordings, guided practice, a community forum, resources, and their own progress in a single login. The cohort of 8 to 15 becomes a support group as much as a classroom, the piece the company credits for turning intentions into routines that outlast the course.

The format is eTeacher's proven engine, refined through years of live teaching to 400,000+ learners across nearly 200 countries and now adapted for healthspan, taught by globally certified longevity instructors, including founding faculty member Julie Gibson Clark, who held the number two spot on the Rejuvenation Olympics leaderboard for more than a year, ahead of the contest's own founder Bryan Johnson. It is the same approach that helped hundreds of thousands of people finish a course they might otherwise have quit, now pointed at the goal of adding strong, capable years to ordinary lives.

The Longevity Blueprint is enrolling now from $289 per month. A companion practice app is on eTeacher's roadmap, intended to bring the same daily, low-pressure guidance into students' pockets, alongside additional longevity courses planned for the year ahead.

"Our goal is to simplify and democratize longevity so anyone, whatever their background, can start their own routine. That is why we put a real teacher in the room and a small class around you, a healthy life is built one taught, repeated habit at a time, and everyone deserves to be taught, supported, and expected back until it sticks," said Harel Tayeb, CEO of eTeacher Group.
